Find a comfortable position with a straight back, and gently close your eyes.

Begin by taking a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to transition from where you were a few moments ago to where you are right now.

Breathe in… and breathe out.

Let your breath become long and natural.

(PAUSE)

Bring your attention to the full length of each inhale… and the full length of each exhale.

Let go of anything that isn’t the breath.

(PAUSE)

Allow your mind to rest on the breath… nothing else.

(PAUSE)

Now gently shift your awareness to your body.

Notice if there is any tightness or tension you may be holding.

Take a deep breath in.

And as you exhale, see if you can soften that tension… just a little.

(SHORT PAUSE)

With each breath out, allow your body to relax more deeply.

Let it feel heavy… like a wave settling.

Relax into this moment.

(LONG PAUSE)

As you sit here, your mind may begin to wander.

You might notice thoughts about your day, or concerns about what’s ahead.

That’s okay.

Allow these thoughts to come and go… without holding onto them.

Watch them drift away, like clouds moving across the sky.

Release your anxiety by releasing the thoughts that create it.

With a calm and clear mind, you are better able to face whatever comes your way.

Now return your attention to the breath.

Follow it all the way in… and all the way out.

(LONG PAUSE)

Notice how everything is constantly changing.

Every situation has a beginning… and an ending.

Just like the breath.

It rises… and it passes.

Rises… and passes.

(LONG PAUSE)

And as this practice comes to an end, slowly begin to bring your awareness back to the room.

Take your time.

When you feel ready, gently open your eyes.

And if you can, carry this sense of calm with you into the rest of your day.
